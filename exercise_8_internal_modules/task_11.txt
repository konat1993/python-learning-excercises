Once upon a time, in a small village at the edge of a dark forest, there lived a young boy named Oliver. He was known for his curiosity and kindness, but also for getting himself into trouble by wandering too far from home.

One evening, as the sun was setting and painting the sky in shades of orange and purple, Oliver heard a faint cry coming from the forest. Despite the villagers’ warnings, he decided to follow the sound. The deeper he went, the quieter the world became, until he found a small, glowing creature trapped under a fallen branch.

It was a forest spirit, no bigger than his hand, with shimmering wings and bright, worried eyes. Oliver carefully lifted the branch and freed the creature. In return, the spirit thanked him and offered a single wish. Oliver thought for a moment, then asked not for gold or fame, but for the forest to always be safe for everyone in the village.

The spirit smiled and granted his wish. From that day on, the forest was no longer feared. It became a place of wonder and peace, where people and magical creatures lived in harmony. And Oliver, who once got lost so easily, became the one others trusted to guide them safely through the trees.
